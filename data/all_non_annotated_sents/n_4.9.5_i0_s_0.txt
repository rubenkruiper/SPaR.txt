Guidance to the Water Byelaws recommends that, to prevent the development of Legionella or similar pathogens, hot water within a storage vessel should be stored at a temperature of not less than 60 degrees C and distributed at a temperature of not less than 55 degrees C. Detailed guidance on the control of Legionella may be found in HSE Approved Code of Practice L8 - `Legionnaires' Disease - Control of Legionella Bacteria in Water Systems'.
